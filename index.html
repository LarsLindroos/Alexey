Thesis Statement: What Makes a Man a Hero?





Heroes can have be just as impactful as a hero in providing morale for a people lacking in hope, or 
faith in their own country, as more classic heroes, known best for their help in saving the lives of others. 
Often, people consider soldiers (like Alexey) much lesser heroes than that of the “Schindler” sort; this isn’t 
necessarily true. According to Merriam-Webster, a hero is defined as such: “A person admired for achievements 
and noble qualities” and “One who shows great courage”. Russia, and its people, were in a great time of need during 
the period of time surrounding WW2; according to bbc, many of the people there lived in fear of their corrupt 
government, and got along on very poor living conditions. In an article by bbc, Kazimiera Pawlinska, a woman who lived 
in Russia during 1940, gave her story. At one point, she said “Looking at the life we led in retrospect, the society 
was constantly afraid and profoundly corrupt”. Soldiers like Alexey represented a powerful, heroic entity aside from 
their corrupt government. Thanks to his books and immense news coverage (within Russia), Alexey Maresyev inspired many 
people throughout his homeland, and helped to restore even a small fraction of their faith in their own country.
